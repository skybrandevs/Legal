Our Principal & his team

The present principal in chambers, Mr. Olatunji Ajose-Adeogun was called to the Nigerian Bar in 1984. Following his call to the Bar, he served as junior counsel in the chambers of Prof A. B. Kasunmu, a Senior Advocate of Nigeria. It was here that he was exposed to litigation practice and corporate law at the highest levels.

Mr. Olatunji Ajose-Adeogun worked for Prof. Kasunmu S.A.N. for a period of five and a half years In Mr. Olatunji Ajose-Adeogun’s time at Prof A. B. Kasunmu’s chambers, a new foundation was laid for the re-engineering of the old firm O.Ajose Adeogun & Co. This exercise commenced in 1988 with the formal registration and change of the firm name to OLAOTAN AJOSE - ADEOGUN & CO.

The Original firm O.Ajose Adeogun & Co was established in 1961 by Mr. Olatunji Ajose-Adeogun who was called to the English Bar (Middle Temple) in 1952. On his arrival from England he practiced with the law firm Thomas, Williams and Kayode, after which he took up employment with the L.E.D.B, which is now the present-day Lagos State Development and Property Corporation. He remained in private legal practice until his elevation as a Judge of the High Court of Lagos State in 1975. In 1985 he was further elevated to the Court of Appeal from where he voluntarily retired in 1987 Mr. Olatunji Ajose-Adeogun is a member of The Chartered Institute of Arbitrators England, a Fellow of the Nigerian Institute of Chartered Arbitrators, a member of the Lagos Court of Arbitration and a Notary Public for Nigeria having been so appointed by the Chief Justice of Nigeria in 1993.

Also present in chambers are Mrs. Duro-Emmanuel (formally Miss Ayo Ajose-Adeogun) called in 1978 and 5 junior counsel / solicitors working for the firm. The firm has support staff such as Chambers Receptionist, Chambers Accountant, Litigation Clerk, cleaners & security.
