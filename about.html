About us.

Conscious of our reputation, which has been built over a period of over 59 years we strive to achieve the highest level of professionalism.

The law firm established in 1961 was re-established and has today become OLAOTAN AJOSE - ADEOGUN & CO. The present principal in chambers is Mr. Olaotan Ajose-Adeogun who was called to the Nigerian Bar in 1984. Following his call to the Bar, he served as junior counsel in the chambers of Prof A. B. Kasunmu, a Senior Advocate of Nigeria. It was here that he was exposed to litigation practice and corporate law at the highest levels. Mr. Olaotan Ajose-Adeogun worked for Prof. Kasunmu S.A.N. for a period of five and a half years

During his stay there, a new foundation was laid for the re-engineering of the old firm O. AJOSE-ADEOGUN & CO. This exercise commenced in 1988 with the formal registration and change of the firm name to OLAOTAN AJOSE - ADEOGUN & CO.

The firm moved to its present location in January 1991 and has also opened a branch office in Port Harcourt as shown on our headed paper. There are also plans to open an office in Abuja. The opening of a branch office has enabled us to provide better professional service to our clients at a competitive cost. The firm’s library is well equipped with relevant practice and precedent books, as well as law reports. It is the policy of the firm to ensure that the library is continuously updated with relevant materials that are acquired locally and from abroad.

On the personnel of the firm the principal in chambers as stated earlier was called to the Bar in 1984. He is a member of The Chartered Institute of Arbitrators England, a Fellow of the Nigerian Institute of Chartered Arbitrators, a member of the Lagos Court of Arbitration and a Notary Public for Nigeria having been so appointed by the Chief Justice of Nigeria in 1993. He has over the past 36 years acquired vast experience in Corporate Law, Commercial Practice including Law of Contract, Law of Real Property including Law of Land Lord and Tennant and the acquisition of property, Banking and Insurance, Debt Recovery, Immigration Law, Labour Law including industrial disputes, Energy & Petroleum Law, Arbitration Law and Proceedings, and General Litigation Practice

Also present in chambers are Mrs. Duro-Emmanuel (formally Miss Ayo Ajose-Adeogun) called in 1978 and 5 junior counsel / solicitors working for the firm. The firm has support staff such as Chambers Receptionist, Chambers Accountant, Litigation Clerk, cleaners and security personal. The firm has clients in the Petroleum Industry, Banking and Insurance, Shipping Industry and several individual and corporate clients in Nigeria and abroad.
